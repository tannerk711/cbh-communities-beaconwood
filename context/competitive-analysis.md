# Competitive Analysis — Homebuilder Community Pages
## Reference for Beaconwood Prototype Design

---

## Builders Studied

- Lennar (lennar.com)
- Toll Brothers (tollbrothers.com)
- Shea Homes (sheahomes.com)
- CBH Homes current pages (cbhhomes.com) — baseline/gap analysis

---

## What the Best Pages Do Well

### 1. Lennar
**Strengths:**
- Clean, uncluttered layouts with strong white space
- "Everything's Included®" branding is powerful — makes the value prop crystal clear
- Floor plan cards are well-designed: image, name, sq ft, beds/baths, price, CTA
- Strong use of lifestyle photography (not just renderings)
- "Interest List" CTA on coming-soon pages — smart email capture
- Quick move-in homes are prominently flagged
- Next Gen® (multigenerational) homes have dedicated callout
- Mobile experience is solid
- Community-specific pages load fast

**Weaknesses:**
- Community pages feel template-stamped — not differentiated per community
- Designer finishes / what's included isn't surfaced on community pages
- Lead forms are generic
- FAQ sections are thin
- No colorway/palette selector

**Key patterns to steal:**
- Quick stats bar (sq ft range, price range, home count)
- "Everything's Included" style value section with icon grid
- Floor plan card design pattern
- Interest list / email capture for pre-launch

---

### 2. Toll Brothers
**Strengths:**
- Premium, luxury aesthetic — photography is exceptional
- Masonry photo gallery is best-in-class
- Community narrative copy is lifestyle-rich and aspirational
- "Design Studio" section on some pages — shows what customization is possible
- Virtual tours and 3D walkthroughs embedded on floor plan pages
- Detailed community amenity sections with icons + descriptions
- Map integration is excellent — shows proximity to key destinations
- Trust signals are prominent: awards, years in business, J.D. Power rankings

**Weaknesses:**
- Pages are heavy — lots of images = slower load times
- Conversion path is long — too many clicks to get to a lead form
- Can feel intimidating / high-pressure for first-time luxury buyers
- Not great at communicating value/included features vs. upgrades

**Key patterns to steal:**
- Aspirational lifestyle narrative section
- Photo gallery quality and layout
- Proximity map with labeled callouts
- Design Studio / finishes showcase concept (this is what the colorway section is inspired by)
- Sales agent profiles with personality

---

### 3. Shea Homes
**Strengths:**
- "Personalization" section on community pages is excellent — shows buyers they have choices
- Clean sticky nav within the page (Overview / Homes / Amenities / Location / Contact)
- Testimonials placed mid-page, not just at bottom
- "Why [Community Name]" section uses icon + headline + short copy format — scannable
- Good use of neighborhood lifestyle imagery (not just houses)

**Weaknesses:**
- Finishes/included features buried or missing from community pages
- Lead capture forms are basic
- Mobile nav is clunky

**Key patterns to steal:**
- In-page sticky anchor navigation
- "Why [Community]" icon + headline + copy block format
- Mid-page testimonials placement
- Personalization/colorway concept

## The Opportunity Summary

CBH's current community pages are functional but leave significant conversion and SEO value on the table. The prototype should leapfrog all three competitors by combining:

1. **Toll Brothers' visual quality** (aspirational photography, rich narrative)
2. **Lennar's clarity** (stats strip, included features, fast load)
3. **Shea's UX patterns** (sticky in-page nav, colorway/personalization section)
4. **CBH's unique advantages** (self-guided tours, rate buydowns, local Idaho identity)

...all built on Astro for superior performance, which itself becomes a ranking advantage.
